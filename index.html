The concept

One syllable. Every country.

NYC slang meets world travel — a format with infinite episodes and zero competition.

The word
"Yerr" — one syllable, infinite connections
NYC slang for "what's up" — rooted in African American Vernacular English, Puerto Rican Spanish, and Yiddish. The city's entire multicultural DNA in one sound. Simple enough for anyone to say, foreign enough to create genuine comedy abroad.
The format
Teach locals your greeting, learn theirs
Every country has their own "yerr" — klk in DR, wagwan in Jamaica, ossu in Japan, g'day in Australia. We teach them ours, they teach us theirs. Infinite episodes, infinite reactions, infinite content.
The gap
Zero NYC Latino travel duos exist
65M+ Latinos in the US, $56B/year spent on travel — and nobody is making travel content from the perspective of two NYC Latinos seeing the world for the first time. We're not competing. We're creating the lane.
The proof
The format already works — at scale
Xiaomanyc (6.4M subs) — NYC guy speaking foreign languages. Kieran Murray — Aussie teaching locals "G'day." Drew Binsky — $114K/mo. Same formula, different culture. Nobody's done it with NYC slang yet.
