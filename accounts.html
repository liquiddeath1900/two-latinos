Social media accounts
Your content platforms — create all with the same handle
Day 1
Lock the handle first. Check all platforms at once — if @TwoLatinos is taken on one, find a handle that's available everywhere. Consistency = brand recognition.
TikTok — Business Account
Growth engine. Algorithm favors new accounts posting daily. Business account gives analytics + link in bio. Post 1-2x daily for the first 30 days.
Priority #1
Instagram — Professional/Creator Account
Hotels check IG first when you pitch. Professional account unlocks insights and the "contact" button. Cross-post best TikToks as Reels. Post 3x/week + daily stories.
Priority #1
YouTube — Brand Account
Long-form money machine. Need 1K subs + 4K watch hours for ad revenue (or 10M Shorts views). Upload weekly vlogs + cross-post TikToks as Shorts.
Priority #1

Not a priority platform for travel content, but reserve the name. Post occasionally for brand presence.

Review & travel platforms
Build your reviewer reputation — hotels and restaurants notice top contributors
Day 1-7
Why this matters: A TripAdvisor "Top Contributor" badge or Google Local Guide Level 5+ is social proof when you pitch hotels. It shows you're a legitimate reviewer, not just someone asking for free stays. Start reviewing everything now.
TripAdvisor — Create account, start reviewing
Review every restaurant and hotel you visit. Detailed reviews with photos. Work toward "Top Contributor" status. Hotels see this when you pitch them.
Both do this
Yelp — Create account, aim for Yelp Elite
Review restaurants with quality photos. Yelp Elite status gets you invited to exclusive food events and tastings — free content. Apply for Elite after 20+ reviews.
Both do this
Google Maps — Level up as Local Guide
Leave reviews + photos on every place you visit. Level 4+ gets Google perks (early access, rewards). Your reviews also appear in Google Search — SEO for free.
Both do this
Booking.com — Create account, start Genius program
Book through Booking.com → Level up to Genius 2/3 → Get room upgrades, discounts, and priority support. Also has an affiliate program for earning on referrals.
Either
Hotels.com — Sign up for Rewards
Every 10 nights booked = 1 free night. Stack this with content trips. Even paid stays earn toward free ones.
Either
Google Travel — Set up flight alerts
google.com/travel — Track prices for destinations you want to visit. Get email alerts when flights drop. Screenshot deals for content ("flights from NYC under $100").

Affiliate & monetization accounts
Start earning from day 1 — no follower minimum on most of these
Week 1
The play: Every video description and bio link should include affiliate links. Someone watches your buffet review, clicks your Booking.com link to check the hotel, and you earn commission. It adds up fast, even with small audiences.
Amazon Associates — Affiliate program
Link your gear in every video description: camera, gimbal, mic, tripod, travel accessories. Commission on everything the person buys (not just what you linked) for 24 hours. No minimum followers.
Day 1
Booking.com Affiliate Partner
Earn commission on every hotel booking through your link. Put your Booking.com affiliate link in every travel video description and on your Linktree.
Day 1
Travelpayouts — Travel affiliate network
One platform, many brands: flights (Kiwi, WayAway), hotels (Booking, Agoda), tours (Viator, GetYourGuide), insurance. Higher payouts than going direct. No minimum.
Week 1
ShareASale — Join travel brand programs
Affiliate network with Viator, TripAdvisor experiences, Priceline, and hundreds of travel brands. Apply to programs individually after joining.
Week 1
Impact.com — Brand partnership platform
Used by Airbnb, Uber, and many hotel chains. Sign up and apply to individual programs. Some require minimum followers, others don't.
Week 2
CapCut — Referral program
If you edit with CapCut (free), include your referral link. Earn when people sign up through you. Easy passive income from "how we edit" content.
