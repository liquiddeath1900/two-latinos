Her Plan

Full body every session — glutes, thighs, upper body, and core mixed together. Every workout includes glute work (3x/week frequency). Only 2-3 upper body exercises per session so wrists never get overloaded. 3-day base, optional 4th day.

The science: Women at 38 can gain 8-12 lbs of lean muscle in year one. Estrogen is still anabolic. Glutes respond best to high frequency — hitting them 3x/week (every session) beats 2x/week. Upper body is spread across all 3 days so wrists only handle 2-3 exercises at a time instead of 7.

Why Full Body 3x/Week?

Better for the goals AND the wrists. Schoenfeld's research: 2-3x/week frequency per muscle is optimal. Full body 3x hits glutes every session. And instead of one brutal upper body day (7 exercises beating up wrists), each session has just 2-3 upper body moves. If wrists flare mid-workout, skip those 2 exercises and keep going — the rest of the workout is still there.

Progressive Overload

The Rule
Step 1: Hit the TOP of the rep range on all sets (e.g., 3×12 when range is 10-12)
Step 2: Next session, add weight (+5 lbs lower body, +2.5 lbs upper body)
Step 3: Drop back to bottom of rep range with new weight
Step 4: Build back up to top of range. Repeat forever.
Realistic timeline: Weeks 1-4 = neurological adaptation. Weeks 8-12 = visible muscle changes. Months 3-6 = peak "newbie gains." ~0.5-1 lb of lean muscle per month.

Posture & Cervical Spine

Why this matters: Cervical spine dysfunction likely connects carpal tunnel AND tinnitus. The nerve glides are built into the warm-up. These extra exercises can be done on rest days or added after any workout.
Chin Tucks — 3 × 10, hold 5 sec Watch →
Pull chin straight back (double chin). Decompresses cervical spine. Do at desk hourly if possible.
Cervical Nerve Glides — 2 × 10 each side Watch →
Tilt head away from affected side, extend arm, open/close hand. Gently — never force into pain.
Prone Y-Raises — 3 × 12 Watch →
Face down on incline bench. Arms in Y, thumbs up. Lift with lower traps. Fixes forward head posture at the root.
Tinnitus connection: If the ringing changes when moving jaw or turning neck — that's somatosensory tinnitus. Cervical PT has shown significant improvement in studies.

Wrist Rehab Protocol

Bonus: The nerve glides are already baked into every warm-up above — so the rehab happens automatically. Doing them 2-3x daily (morning + before bed) would speed things up even more. Full protocol below.

Median Nerve Glides

5-10 REPS EACH HAND
1. Fist — wrist neutral, fingers and thumb curled Watch →
2. Fingers straight — wrist neutral (table-top)
3. Fingers and wrist extended back
4. Same + thumb extended out
5. Same + forearm supinated (palm up)
6. Same + gentle opposite-hand stretch on thumb
Flow through 1→6 slowly. Never force into pain.

Tendon Glides

5-10 REPS
1. Straight fingers (full extension) Watch →
2. Hook fist (bend at middle + end joints, base straight)
3. Full fist
4. Table-top (bend at base joints only)
5. Straight fist (bend at base + middle joints)
Hold each position 3-5 seconds. Smooth transitions.
Night splints: Wear neutral-position wrist splints every night. Cochrane review confirmed they improve CT symptoms. Use wrist wraps (not splints) during workouts.

Sleep Protocol

70% of growth hormone is released during deep sleep. Fixing sleep is directly tied to muscle gain at 38.

500-1,000 mg/day
Fruiting body extract, standardized. Take with dinner. Works through anxiety/stress reduction and nerve growth factor. Start at 500 mg, titrate to 1,000 mg over 2 weeks.

200-400 mg before bed
Activates parasympathetic nervous system, regulates melatonin, binds GABA receptors. Start at 200 mg, work up to 400 mg.

300-600 mg with dinner
Root extract standardized to 5% withanolides. Reduces cortisol ~20% at 60 days — less muscle breakdown, better recovery.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Gripping too hard
If hands are white-knuckled, you're compressing the median nerve. Versa Gripps + wraps every session.
Skipping the workout because wrists hurt
Every workout has 5+ exercises that need zero grip. Drop the 2 upper body moves if wrists are bad — the rest of the session is still a full workout.
Not eating enough
You can't build muscle in a deficit. 2,350 calories is the target. See the Eating Guide.
Expecting overnight results
Visible changes start week 8-12. Peak newbie gains months 3-6. Realistic year 1: 8-10 lbs of lean muscle.
