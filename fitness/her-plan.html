Carpal Tunnel Rules (Every Session)

Every exercise is CT-modified. Neutral grips, straps for pulling, wraps for pressing, machines where possible. No heavy barbell gripping, no wrist curls, no power cleans. These protect the median nerve while still building muscle.

Progressive Overload

The Rule
Step 1: Hit the TOP of the rep range on all sets (e.g., 3×12 when range is 10-12)
Step 2: Next session, add weight (+5 lbs lower body, +2.5 lbs upper body)
Step 3: Drop back to bottom of rep range with new weight
Step 4: Build back up to top of range. Repeat forever.
Realistic timeline: Weeks 1-4 = neurological adaptation (stronger fast, but that's your brain, not muscle yet). Weeks 8-12 = visible muscle changes begin. Months 3-6 = peak "newbie gains." Month by month: ~0.5-1 lb of lean muscle.

Posture & Cervical Spine

Why this matters: Cervical spine dysfunction likely connects her carpal tunnel AND tinnitus. These exercises are built INTO the warm-ups above so they happen automatically. If she wants extra credit, add these on rest days — but the warm-ups already cover the essentials.
Chin Tucks — 3 × 10, hold 5 sec Watch →
Pull chin straight back (make a double chin). Decompresses cervical spine. Do at desk hourly if possible. Zero equipment.
Cervical Nerve Glides — 2 × 10 each side Watch →
Tilt head away from affected side, extend arm, open/close hand. Glides the nerve from neck through arm. Gently — never force into pain.
Prone Y-Raises — 3 × 12 Watch →
Lie face down on incline bench. Arms in Y, thumbs up. Lift with lower traps. Fixes forward head posture at the root.
Tinnitus connection: If the ringing changes when she moves her jaw or turns her neck — that's somatosensory tinnitus. Cervical PT (these exercises + manual therapy) has shown significant improvement in studies.

Wrist Rehab Protocol

Bonus: The nerve glides are already baked into every warm-up above — so the rehab happens automatically. Doing them 2-3x daily (morning + before bed) would speed things up even more. Full protocol below.

Median Nerve Glides

5-10 REPS EACH HAND
1. Fist — wrist neutral, fingers and thumb curled Watch →
2. Fingers straight — wrist neutral (table-top)
3. Fingers and wrist extended back
4. Same + thumb extended out
5. Same + forearm supinated (palm up)
6. Same + gentle opposite-hand stretch on thumb
Flow through positions 1→6 slowly. Never force into pain.

Tendon Glides

5-10 REPS
1. Straight fingers (full extension) Watch →
2. Hook fist (bend at middle + end joints, base straight)
3. Full fist
4. Table-top (bend at base joints only)
5. Straight fist (bend at base + middle joints)
Hold each position 3-5 seconds. Smooth transitions.
Night splints: Wear neutral-position wrist splints every night. A Cochrane review confirmed they improve CT symptoms. Keep wrist at 0-15° extension. Use wrist wraps (not splints) during workouts.

Sleep Protocol

70% of growth hormone is released during deep sleep. Fixing sleep isn't optional — it's directly tied to muscle gain and recovery at 38.

Lion's Mane
500-1,000 mg/day
Fruiting body extract, standardized. Take with dinner. Not a sedative — works through anxiety/stress reduction and nerve growth factor (NGF). Studies showed improved sleep quality after 4 weeks. Start at 500 mg, titrate to 1,000 mg over 2 weeks.
Magnesium Glycinate
200-400 mg before bed
Activates parasympathetic nervous system, regulates melatonin, binds GABA receptors. Start at 200 mg, work up to 400 mg. If stools get loose = back off the dose.
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
300-600 mg with dinner
Root extract standardized to 5% withanolides. Reduces cortisol ~20% at 60 days — less muscle breakdown, better recovery. A 2025 study on female athletes found significant improvement.
Optional Additions
L-Theanine + Tart Cherry
L-Theanine: 200 mg before bed. Alpha brain waves, no grogginess.
Tart cherry juice: 8 oz before bed. Natural melatonin + anti-inflammatory (also helps CT).
Start one at a time. Add each supplement 1 week apart. Lion's mane first → magnesium week 2 → ashwagandha week 3.

Anti-Inflammatory Nutrition (CT Support)

Add to Diet

Turmeric/curcumin: 500-1,000 mg/day with black pepper — neuroprotective
Vitamin B6: 50-100 mg/day. Deficiency linked to CTS. Do not exceed 200 mg — neurotoxic.

Reduce / Avoid
Refined sugar + processed foods — promote inflammation
Excess sodium — increases fluid retention → more swelling in carpal tunnel
Excessive alcohol — neurotoxic, disrupts sleep

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Gripping too hard
If your hands are white-knuckled, you're compressing the median nerve. Straps and wraps every session, not just when it hurts.
Skipping the workout because wrists hurt
That's what the Wrist Flare-Up Swap Day is for. 2 of 3 base workouts are lower body — barely need your hands. Don't let wrists be the excuse to skip entirely.
Not eating enough
You can't build muscle in a deficit. 2,350 calories is the target — not 1,600, not 1,800. See the Eating Guide.
Expecting overnight results
Visible changes start week 8-12. Peak newbie gains are months 3-6. Realistic year 1: 8-10 lbs of lean muscle. Trust the process.
