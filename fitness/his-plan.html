Your Solo Finishers

After the Core Four you do together, these are YOUR moves. Upper mass, width, V-taper, and midsection tightening. 3 sets of 12-15 reps each.

Upper Chest — Prevent Sag
Incline Dumbbell Press Watch →
Bench at 30-45°. 3 × 12, 25-40 lb each. Hits the upper chest specifically — fills out the area most prone to sagging during weight loss. Squeeze at the top.
V-Taper — Width
Lat Pulldowns Watch →
Wide grip, pull to upper chest (not behind neck). 3 × 12. Creates the V-taper look — wide back, narrow waist illusion. Lean back slightly, squeeze lats at bottom.
Arms — Fill Them Out
Hammer Curls Watch →
3 × 12, 20-30 lb. Thumbs-up grip. Hits the brachialis (outer arm) — makes arms look thicker from the front. No swinging, control the negative.
Midsection — Tighten Up
Hanging Knee Raises Watch →
3 × 10-12. Hang from a bar, bring knees to chest. Slow and controlled — no swinging. Tightens the lower abs and builds the foundation under the midsection.
Shoulders — Cap Them Off
Lateral Raises Watch →
3 × 15, 15-20 lb. Slow on the way up AND down. Builds the shoulder caps that make the V-taper pop. Light weight, high reps — ego check this one.
Posture — Pull It All Together
Face Pulls Watch →
3 × 15. Cable at face height, pull to ears with external rotation. Fixes the rounded-shoulder look and balances out all the pressing. Non-negotiable.

Weekly Progression

The rule: Every week, try to add 5% more weight OR 1 more rep to each exercise. Don't jump weight until you can do all 3 sets at the top of the rep range with solid form. Ego lifting = injuries = setbacks.

Skin Management Strategy

The Real Talk
You already know the deal. At 285, significant weight loss will likely mean some loose skin. But you're doing everything right to minimize it. Here's the protocol:

1. Slow & Steady Wins

1-2 lbs per week MAX. Losing faster = skin can't keep up = more sagging. The 500-cal deficit is your speed limit. Trust the process.

2. Fill the Frame

This is why the lifting matters more than cardio. Muscle gives skin something to wrap around. Every pound of muscle you build while losing fat = less "empty" skin. Your finishers (incline press, lat pulldowns) specifically target the areas most affected.

3. Skin Health Protocol

  • Hydration: Minimum 100 oz water daily. Skin elasticity depends on it.
  • Collagen peptides: Add to your morning coffee or shake. Supports skin repair.
  • Vitamin C & E: Citrus fruits, berries, spinach, avocados — all on the grocery list.
  • Moisturize daily: Cocoa butter or collagen lotion on chest, arms, midsection. Won't prevent it entirely, but helps elasticity and comfort.

4. The Surgery Timeline

Most surgeons say: maintain your goal weight for 6-12 months before pursuing panniculectomy or abdominoplasty. This gives skin maximum time to tighten naturally. Some areas will surprise you — arms and chest often tighten more than expected when there's muscle underneath.
